Target Audience

  • Business owners: SACCI represents the interests of businesses in South Africa, so it is likely that business owners would be a key audience for the website. These individuals would be interested in information and resources related to policy and legislative development, international trade, taxation, economic affairs, labor and company wellness, education and training, information technology, small business, transport, and regulatory affairs.
  • Industry professionals: In addition to business owners, industry professionals who work in fields related to the above areas would also be a target audience for the SACCI website. These individuals may be interested in networking opportunities and staying up-to-date on the latest policy and regulatory developments that impact their industries.

Personas

John

John is a small business owner in South Africa who is interested in finding ways to grow his business and improve his bottom line. He often struggles to keep up with changes in policy and regulations, and is looking for a reliable source of information and support to help him navigate these challenges.

Scenarios:

  • John is considering expanding his business into a new market, but is unsure about the tax implications of doing so. He visits the SACCI website and finds a detailed guide to international trade regulations, as well as information about tax incentives and credits for businesses operating in certain markets.
  • John is struggling to find qualified employees to fill open positions in his business. He visits the SACCI website and finds a section dedicated to education and training, which includes resources for businesses looking to upskill their employees and connect with local training providers.
  • John has heard that there may be changes to the regulatory environment that could impact his business. He visits the SACCI website and finds a section dedicated to regulatory affairs, which includes news and updates about pending legislation, as well as information about how businesses can get involved in the policy-making process.

Sarah

Sarah is a mid-level executive in a large corporation in South Africa. She is responsible for staying up-to-date on policy and regulatory changes that impact her company, and is often called upon to provide guidance and insights to senior leadership. She is interested in networking with other industry professionals and staying informed about best practices and emerging trends in her field.

Scenarios:

  • Sarah is attending an industry conference and is interested in networking with other professionals in her field. She visits the SACCI website and finds a calendar of upcoming events, as well as a directory of member businesses that she can use to connect with other professionals in her industry.
  • Sarah is tasked with developing a new policy position on labor and company wellness for her company. She visits the SACCI website and finds a detailed whitepaper on the subject, as well as a list of recommended experts and consultants who can provide additional guidance.
  • Sarah is tasked with finding a new transportation provider for her company's logistics operations. She visits the SACCI website and finds a directory of member businesses in the transport sector, as well as reviews and ratings from other businesses who have used their services.
